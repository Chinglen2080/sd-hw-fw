Did You Know?

● Paro is home to Bhutan's **sole international airport (PBH)**, making it the country's primary gateway for international visitors.

● The sacred **Taktsang Goemba (Tiger's Nest)** clings dramatically to a cliff face 900 meters above the Paro Valley floor, an essential pilgrimage site.

● The **Paro Tshechu**, held annually in the spring, is one of Bhutan's most important religious festivals, famous for its sacred masked dances.

Local Crafts & Arts

  • ✓ **Thangka Painting:** Detailed religious scroll paintings essential for meditation.
  • ✓ **Pangkheb Weaving:** Intricate woven aprons and textiles using vibrant, traditional Bhutanese patterns.
  • ✓ **Cham (Mask Dances):** Elaborate, highly stylized sacred dances performed by monks and laymen during festivals.

Traditional Foods

  • ✓ **Ema Datshi:** Bhutan's national dish; a spicy stew of chili peppers and local cheese.
  • ✓ **Jasha Maroo:** A spicy chicken stew often made with ginger, garlic, and chilies, served with rice.
  • ✓ **Red Rice:** The staple grain of the region, known for its deep color, nutty flavor, and soft, slightly chewy texture.

Etc (Key Transportation)

Paro is critical for connectivity as it hosts the **Paro International Airport (PBH)**, the only airfield in Bhutan capable of handling international jet traffic. The approach is considered one of the most challenging in the world, requiring highly specialized pilot training.

Places of Interest

Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest)

The iconic monastery built on a sheer cliff face. It is an essential stop and requires a strenuous hike up the mountain.

Rinpung Dzong

Also known as the 'Fortress on a Heap of Jewels,' this massive fortress houses the district government office and monastic body.

Kyichu Lhakhang

One of the oldest and most sacred temples in Bhutan, said to have been built in the 7th century by the Tibetan King Songtsen Gampo.
