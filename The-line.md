Report on Paro (Bhutan)# DetailsDetailInformationPopulation (2017)~46,316 (Paro District)LanguageDzongkha (National language of Bhutan)State it is present in (if any)Paro is a Dzongkhag (District), which is the primary administrative division in Bhutan.Current LeaderThe head of the administration is the Dzongdag (District Administrator). (Most recently reported Dzongdag is Dasho Karma Thinley, as of August 2021).# Did you know?FactDescriptionFact oneParo is home to Paro International Airport (VDPB), Bhutan's only international airport, renowned for being one of the most challenging airports in the world to land at due to its mountainous approach.Fact twoThe valley was historically the closest cultural and geographical connection point to Tibet, serving as the main route for trade goods and historical invasions.Fact threeThe massive Rinpung Dzong (Paro Dzong) served as a principal location for the filming of the 1993 movie, Little Buddha.# Local Crafts and Performance ArtsArt FormDescriptionArt one: Par Zo (Carving)The traditional craft of carving, applied extensively to wood (e.g., masks for festivals, window frames, pillars) and slate.Art two: Lhazo (Painting)The art of painting, used for creating religious murals on monastery walls and intricately detailed Thangkas (scroll paintings) of deities.Art three: Chham (Masked Dance)Sacred dance performances where monks or laymen wear elaborate masks and costumes, performed during the annual Tshechu religious festivals.# Traditional FoodsFood NameDescriptionFood one: Ema DatshiBhutan's national dish. A fiery stew made primarily from large green or red chilies (ema) cooked with local Bhutanese cheese (datshi).Food two: Kewa DatshiA milder, potato-based variation of Datshi, where sliced potatoes (kewa) are cooked with cheese and sometimes a smaller amount of chili.Food three: HoentayA type of traditional buckwheat dumpling, usually filled with turnip leaves, cheese, or minced meat, often prepared during specific festivals.# Place of InterestPlace NameSignificancePlace one: Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest)The most iconic site in Bhutan, a sacred monastery complex clinging to the side of a cliff, where Guru Padmasambhava is said to have meditated.Place two: Rinpung DzongA large dzong (fortress-monastery) that serves as the administrative seat of the district government and houses the monastic body.Place three: Kyichu LhakhangAn ancient Buddhist temple that is one of the oldest in Bhutan, believed to have been built in the 7th century.# EtcThe National Museum of Bhutan (Ta Dzong) is located in Paro, offering a repository of Bhutanese history, artifacts, and culture.The area is a popular starting point for treks, including the route to the famous Jomolhari mountain.Paro is the gateway to the beautiful Haa Valley via the high Chele La Pass.
