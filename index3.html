# Did You Know?

  • ✪

    Paro is home to **Bhutan's sole international airport** (VDPB), famous for its challenging approach through the Himalayas.

  • ✪

    Rinpung Dzong (Paro Dzong) was used as a filming location for several scenes in the Bernardo Bertolucci film, ***Little Buddha***.

  • ✪

    The Paro Valley historically served as the **primary invasion and trade route** from Tibet, giving it deep cultural connections to the north.

# Local Crafts and Performance Arts

Art 1: Par Zo (Carving)

The traditional art of carving, especially in wood and slate, is central to Paro, seen in the decorative masks for Tshechu festivals and the ornamentation of traditional houses and Dzongs.

Art 2: Lhazo (Painting)

This involves creating intricate religious wall murals in monasteries and *Thangkas* (religious scroll paintings) used for display during annual festivals.

Art 3: Chham (Masked Dance)

The sacred masked dances performed by monks and laymen during the annual *Tshechu* festivals, which are a major spiritual and cultural event.

Food 2: Kewa Datshi

A comfort dish consisting of sliced potatoes (*kewa*) cooked in a cheese sauce (*datshi*), often served with a milder chili level than its Ema counterpart.

Food 3: Hoentay

Buckwheat dumplings traditionally filled with a mix of turnip greens and cheese. They are a delicacy often associated with special occasions.

# Places of Interest

  • Place 1: Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest)

    The iconic monastery perched precariously on a cliffside, founded on the site where Guru Padmasambhava is believed to have meditated.

  • Place 2: Rinpung Dzong

    Meaning "Fortress on a Heap of Jewels," this large, striking fortress houses the district administrative offices and the monastic body.

  • Place 3: Kyichu Lhakhang

    One of Bhutan's oldest and most sacred temples, dating back to the 7th century, said to be built to pin down the left foot of a demoness.

# Etc (Additional Insights)

  • The **National Museum of Bhutan** is located in Paro, housed in the former *Ta Dzong* (Watchtower) above the Rinpung Dzong.
  • The Paro River (*Paro Chhu*) flows through the valley, meeting the Wang Chhu (Thimphu River) downstream.
  • **Chele La Pass**, one of Bhutan's highest motorable passes, is a popular side trip from Paro, offering panoramic views of the Jomolhari mountain.
