Paro, Kingdom of Bhutan Destination Report

Paro is a historic town and the administrative seat of the Paro District (Dzongkhag) in the Kingdom of Bhutan. It is located in the beautiful, wide Paro Valley and is culturally significant as the site of Bhutan's only international airport and the iconic Tiger's Nest Monastery.

Did You Know?

Fact One: Paro is home to Bhutan's sole international airport (PBH), making it the country's primary gateway for all international visitors.

Fact Two: The sacred Taktsang Goemba (Tiger's Nest Monastery) clings dramatically to a sheer cliff face 900 meters above the Paro Valley, an essential pilgrimage site.

Fact Three: The annual Paro Tshechu, a major religious festival held in the spring, is famous for its elaborate sacred masked dances (Cham) and the unfolding of a massive thangka scroll.

Local Crafts and Performance Arts

Art One - Thangka Painting: Detailed religious scroll paintings that depict Buddhist deities, mandalas, or life stories. These are highly skilled, precise works used for spiritual practice.

Art Two - Pangkheb Weaving: The weaving of intricate textiles, particularly the Pangkheb (aprons) and the raw silk fabrics used for the national dress (Gho for men, Kira for women).

Art Three - Cham (Sacred Mask Dances): An integral part of the Paro Tshechu, these dances are performed by monks and laymen to convey moral lessons and bless the audience.

Traditional Foods

Food One - Ema Datshi: Considered the national dish of Bhutan. It is a fiery stew made primarily of large, local chili peppers and local cheese.

Food Two - Jasha Maroo: A popular, spicy chicken stew typically prepared with minced or diced chicken, ginger, garlic, and chilies, served hot with a side of rice.

Food Three - Red Rice: The staple grain for many Bhutanese, this nutritious rice is distinguished by its reddish hue, nutty flavor, and soft, slightly chewy texture.

Place of Interest

Place One - Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest Monastery): The most famous landmark in Bhutan, a spectacular monastery complex associated with Guru Rinpoche, who is credited with introducing Buddhism to Bhutan.

Place Two - Rinpung Dzong: A massive, impressive fortress that serves as the administrative center for the district. The annual Tshechu festival is held in its courtyard.

Place Three - Kyichu Lhakhang: Believed to be one of the 108 temples built by the Tibetan King Songtsen Gampo in the 7th century, making it one of the oldest and most revered shrines in the country.

Etc (Key Transportation)

Paro's most vital feature is its role as the country's primary transport hub. The Paro International Airport (PBH) is the only airfield in Bhutan capable of handling commercial jet traffic, situated deep within the Himalayan valleys. It is renowned globally for its technically demanding approach and spectacular scenery.
